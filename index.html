Monsters, Inc. is about monsters who scare children to generate energy. When a human girl accidentally enters their world, two monsters, Sulley and Mike, try to help her get back home, discovering that laughter can be more powerful than fear.

Sulley

Sulley, also known as James P. Sullivan, is a big, furry, blue monster with horns and a gentle personality. He's one of the top employees at Monsters, Inc., holding the position of the company's top scarer. Despite his intimidating appearance, Sulley is friendly, compassionate, and has a huge heart. He highly values his friendships, especially with his partner Mike, and demonstrates a strong sense of responsibility and leadership. Throughout the movie, Sulley undergoes an emotional journey as he develops a special bond with the little human girl, Boo, and learns the importance of laughter and empathy over fear.

Mike Wazowski

Mike Wazowski is a small, lime-green, one-eyed monster with a spherical shape. He works alongside Sulley at Monsters, Inc. and is Sulley's best friend and roommate. Despite his diminutive size, Mike has a confident and outgoing personality. He's ambitious, determined, and dreams of becoming a top scarer like Sulley. Mike is quick-witted, enthusiastic, and often serves as the comic relief in various situations. He's fiercely loyal to his friends and possesses a big heart, even if his physical appearance is quite the opposite. Throughout the story, Mike showcases his determination and resourcefulness, proving that bravery comes in all shapes and sizes.

Boo

Boo is a sweet, innocent, and curious little girl who accidentally enters the monster world. She's around two years old, with short black hair and a penchant for laughter and playfulness. Despite initially being scared of the monsters, particularly Sulley and Mike, Boo quickly develops a strong bond with them. She's incredibly endearing, with her adorable mannerisms and her tendency to refer to Sulley as "Kitty." Boo's innocence and affectionate nature play a significant role in the story, showing the monsters the power of love, kindness, and laughter, ultimately transforming their perspective on scaring children.

Randall

Randall Boggs is a villainous, purple chameleon monster in "Monsters, Inc." He's competitive, cunning, and always looking for ways to climb the corporate ladder. Randall is the main antagonist in the film, willing to do whatever it takes to achieve his goals, including aggressively scaring children to collect more screams and gain recognition. His chameleon-like ability makes him dangerous as he can easily camouflage and become nearly invisible. His obsession with being the top scarer leads him into confrontations with Sulley and Mike, sparking the main conflicts in the story.

Waternoose

Waternoose is an important character in "Monsters, Inc." He is the CEO of Monsters, Inc., a giant crab with an imposing appearance. Initially portrayed as a respectable leader concerned about the company's well-being, he aims to keep Monsters, Inc. profitable. However, later in the story, he's revealed as one of the main antagonists. He makes extreme decisions to keep the company running, even if it means scaring children excessively. His desperate quest for energy leads him to take drastic measures, challenging morality to ensure the company's success.
